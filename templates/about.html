⚖️ About सत्यवाणी

Our mission, values, and the mythology that guides us

Our Mission — हाम्रो उद्देश्य

SatyaVani (सत्यवाणी — "Voice of Truth") was born from the belief that corruption thrives in silence and dies in the light of truth. Inspired by the Vedic principle of Satya (truth) and the Dharmaraj Yama — the cosmic arbiter of justice — we built a secure platform where every Nepali citizen can speak truth to power without fear.

Nepal's CIAA (Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority) handles corruption but citizens lack a direct, anonymous, and safe channel. SatyaVani fills this gap.

The Mythology Behind SatyaVani

🔱 The Trishul

Shiva's trident represents the three piercing forces of our platform: Anonymity (protection), Truth (accuracy), and Justice (action). The Trishul destroys evil — we use it against the evil of corruption.

🪷 The Lotus

The lotus rises from mud to bloom in purity. SatyaVani rises from the corruption of society to bring forth pure, unfiltered truth — just as Lakshmi and Saraswati stand upon the lotus, so truth stands above corruption.

👁 The Third Eye

Shiva's Netra (third eye) sees through all illusion and deception. Our verification process is the third eye — piercing through fabrication to find the truth within each submission.

🦅 Garuda

Garuda is the fearless eagle who carries Vishnu — the maintainer of cosmic order. Whistleblowers are the modern Garuda — they carry truth swiftly, courageously, and in the face of danger.

⚖️ Dharmaraj

Yama, the Dharmaraj, maintains the cosmic record of all deeds — the Chitragupta's ledger. SatyaVani maintains the earthly ledger of corrupt deeds, ensuring accountability in this world as Yama ensures it in the next.

🐍 The Serpents

In the Mahabharata, Garuda is the eternal enemy of serpents. Corruption is the serpent — coiled around institutions, poisoning society. Every whistleblower is a Garuda striking at these serpents.
