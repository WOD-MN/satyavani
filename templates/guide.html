🌐 Digital Safety

  • Use Tor Browser — Available free at torproject.org. It hides your IP address and encrypts your traffic through multiple layers.
  • Use a VPN — A trusted VPN adds an additional layer of protection even if you're not using Tor.
  • Public WiFi — Submit from a coffee shop or library WiFi that has no connection to your identity.
  • Incognito Mode — Always use private/incognito browsing when accessing this platform.
  • Disable JavaScript — For maximum security, use the NoScript browser extension.

📱 Device Safety

  • Don't use your work device — Your employer may monitor work devices.
  • Don't use your personal phone — Use a borrowed or temporary device when possible.
  • Disable location services — Turn off GPS and location on any device used.
  • Clear browser history — After submitting, clear all history, cookies, and cache.

📎 Collecting Evidence Safely

  • Photograph documents without including your reflection or identifiable background.
  • Screen recordings — Record screens showing digital evidence.
  • We strip metadata — But to be safe, manually remove metadata from files before uploading using tools like MAT2 or ExifTool.
  • Don't email evidence to yourself — Email leaves a trail. Use physical USB drives.

🧅 Using Tor Browser

  1. Download Tor Browser from torproject.org
  2. Install and launch it
  3. Navigate to this website's address
  4. Submit your report normally
  5. Close Tor Browser completely after

Tor encrypts your connection through 3 relays worldwide. Even we cannot see your IP address when you use Tor.

⚖️ Legal Protections in Nepal

  • Nepal's Whistleblower Protection Act provides legal protections for those who report corruption in good faith.
  • The Right to Information Act protects journalists and citizens seeking government information.
  • The CIAA Act allows citizens to file complaints about abuse of authority.
  • Contact FWEAN (Forum for Women Law and Development) or INSEC for legal support.

🔐 After Submission

  • Save your token — Write it on paper, never in a digital note.
  • Don't tell anyone — Including trusted colleagues, until you know it's safe.
  • Watch for retaliation — Document any threats or suspicious behavior after submission.
  • Contact a journalist — Nepali journalists at major outlets (Kantipur, The Himalayan Times, Setopati) can provide additional protection through publication.
