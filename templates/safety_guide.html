Whistleblower Safety Guide सुरक्षा मार्गदर्शन

Protect yourself before, during, and after submitting a report

Before Submitting

  • Use Tor Browser from the Tor Project website
  • Use a public WiFi network, not your home/office internet
  • Use a device that is not registered to your name if possible
  • Never tell anyone you plan to report
  • Remove metadata from files using MAT2 or ExifTool
  • Don't use your personal phone for photos — buy a disposable SIM

While Writing

  • Do NOT include your own name, address, or contact info
  • Avoid details that uniquely identify you as the source
  • Paraphrase quotes rather than using exact words that could identify you
  • Be careful with dates/times if you were the only witness
  • Use general terms ("a senior official") rather than specific titles

For Files & Evidence

  • Strip EXIF/metadata from photos before uploading
  • Remove author info from Word/PDF documents
  • Consider photographing documents rather than scanning (on anonymous device)
  • Audio recordings: use a dedicated device with no SIM card
  • Videos: disable location services before recording

After Submitting

  • Save your token in a secure offline location (paper is safest)
  • Clear browser history and cookies
  • Use incognito/private mode for all follow-up visits
  • Do not discuss the report with colleagues or friends
  • Monitor for unusual activity around you
  • Know your legal rights as a whistleblower in Nepal

🇳🇵 Nepal Whistleblower Legal Protection

Nepal's Corruption Prevention Act 2002 and the Prevention of Corruption Act provide some protections for those who report corruption. The CIAA (Commission for Investigation of Abuse of Authority) is the primary anti-corruption body in Nepal.

If you face retaliation for reporting corruption, contact:

  • CIAA Nepal: (+977) 1-4211761 | ciaa.gov.np
  • NVC Nepal: nvc.gov.np
  • Human Rights Commission: nhrc.org.np
  • Accountability Lab Nepal: accountabilitylab.org
